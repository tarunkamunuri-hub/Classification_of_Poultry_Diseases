Poultry birds, especially chickens, are often affected by infectious diseases that can spread quickly and cause serious problems if not treated early. Fowl Pox is a viral disease that causes wart-like sores or scabs on the comb, beak, and face of the bird. It usually spreads through mosquitoes or direct contact and can be prevented with vaccines. Newcastle Disease is another viral infection that causes symptoms like twisted necks, breathing problems, and paralysis. It spreads through the air or contaminated feed, and although there's no cure, vaccines can help control it. Avian Influenza, also known as bird flu, is a highly contagious virus that causes swelling, bluish combs, and severe illness. It spreads rapidly and can cause high death rates in poultry farms. Detecting these diseases early using image-based AI tools can help farmers take quick action and reduce bird loss.

Newcastle Disease

Newcastle Disease is a dangerous viral infection causing symptoms like twisted necks, paralysis, respiratory issues, and reduced egg production. It spreads through air or contaminated feed. Vaccination is essential, as there's no cure for this disease.

Avian Influenza

Also known as bird flu, Avian Influenza is a contagious virus causing swelling, diarrhea, and a bluish comb. It spreads very fast and can wipe out entire flocks if not detected early. Strict biosecurity and early diagnosis are key to control.
